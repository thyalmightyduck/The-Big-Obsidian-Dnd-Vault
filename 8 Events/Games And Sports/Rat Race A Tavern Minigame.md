# Rat Race
In The Dimly Lit Corners Of Taverns, Patrons Gather Around Crude Wooden Tracks, Cheering As Tiny Rats Each With Their Own Scrappy Charm-Dash Toward The Finish Line. Rat Race Is A Fast Paced Gambling Game Where Players Bet On Which Rat Will Cross The Line First. The Thrill Comes From Not Only The Roll Of The Dice But Also From Betting Against Unpredictable Odds.

“ *Ain’t No Science To It, Friend_ Just Guts, Gold, And A Bit O’ Luck, But If You’re Feelin Brave, Put Your Coin On Ol’ Squacky. Lost His Tale In A Fire, But That Rat’s Faster Than A Rogue On Payday.* ” 
\- **Tarn The Toothless**, “Veteran” Gambler   

## Game Setup
The Game Features Four Distinct Rats, Each Assigned A Number From **1** To **4**. Before The Race Begins, The Tavern’s Bookkeeper Sets The Odds By Rolling **4d6**. Each Result Corresponds To A Rat’s *Pre-Race Performance Predictions*. The More Frequently A Rat’s Number Appears, The More Likely It Is To Win *But With Lower Payouts*.

The Table Below Summarizes How The Odds Are Determines Based On The Pre-Race Roll:

| **Occurrences (4d6)** | **Odds**  | Payout Ratio |
| :-------------------: | :-------: | :----------: |
|           0           | Long Shot |     5:1      |
|           1           | Underdog  |     3:1      |
|           2           | Contender |     2:1      |
|          3+           | Favorite  |     1:1      |

After Rolling And Calculating The Odds, The Bookkeeper Announces Them To The Players Before Any Bets Are Placed.

## Placing Bets
Players Place Their Wagers After Hearing The Odds. They May Bet Any Amount Of Gold They Wish, Depending On The Stakes Allowed By The House. Once All Bets Are Places, The Race Begins.

## The Race Begins
The Bookkeeper Rolls **1d4** To Determine Which Rat Advances In Each Round. The Number Rolled Corresponds To The Rat’s Number. Each Successful Roll Moves The Corresponding Rat One Space Forward On The Imaginary Track.

The First Rat To Advance **Five** Spaces Wins The Race. If Two Or More rats Reach Five Spaces In The Same Round, A Sudden-Death Runoff begins, And The Tied Rats Continue Racing Until A Clear Winner Emerges.

## Payouts And Winnings
Players Who Wagered On The Winning Rat Receive Gold According To The Payout Odds. The Payout Is Calculated By Multiplying. The Player’s Initial Bet By The Rat’s Odds. All Other Players Lose Their Wagers.
- **Formula:** Stake × (Numerator / Denominator) = Profit

## House Rules And Variants
Some Taverns Are Known For Spicing Up The Game With Magical Enhancements Or Introducing “Cheating” Mechanics. DMs Are Encouraged To Add Roleplay Opportunities, Such A *[[Sleight Of Hand]]* Checks For Underhanded Players Or *[[Insight]]* Checks To Spot Rigged Odds.

Additionally, Enchanted Rats May Occasionally Appear, Allowing Players To Place side Bets On Magical Events Occurring During The Race. For Example:
- A Glowing Rat Could Grant Double Payouts If It Wins.
- A Cursed Rt May Lose A Space If A Natural 1 Is Rolled On Any Turn.

## Example Race
**Pre-Race Odds:** The Bookkeeper Rolls 4d6, Resulting In **1,2,3,3.** Odds Are Then Calculated As Follows:
- **Rat 1:** 3:1 
- **Rat 2:** 3:1
- **Rat 3:** 2:1
- **Rat 4:** 5:1 (*No Appearances In The Roll*) 

**Bets Placed:**
- Player **A** Wagers 10gp On **Rat 3**.
- Player **B** Wagers 5gp On **Rat 1**.
- Player **C** Wagers 2gp On **Rat 4**.

**The Race:**
- **Round 1:** A Roll Of 3 Moves **Rat 3** Forward.
- **Round 2:** A Roll Of 4 Advances **Rat 4**.
- **Round 3:** Another 3 Brings **Rat 3** Ahead Again.
- **Round 4:** A Roll Of 1 Gives **Rat 1** A Lead.
- **Round 5:** A Third Roll Of 3 Pushes **Rat 3** Even Closer.
- **Round 6:** One More 3 Secures **Rat 3’s** Victory.

**Payout:** Player **A** Wins With **Rat 3** At 2:1 Odds And Receives 20 GP. Players **B** And **C** Lose Their Wagers.
