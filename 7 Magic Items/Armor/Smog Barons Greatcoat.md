> ##### Description
Perpetually covered in a thin layer of industrial soot, this sturdy greatcoat doubles as a suit of “+2 studded leather” when worn without other armor beneath it. Regardless, you have resistance to fire and poison damage while wearing the greatcoat. Hidden vents line the coat and periodically belch out waste from the surrounding air: you are immune to the effects of harmful gases and vapors (such as “cloudkill” and “stinking cloud” effects, inhaled poisons, and the breath weapons of some dragons).

The coat has 5 charges for the following properties, and it regains 1d3 + 2 expended charges daily at dawn. While the coat has at least 1 remaining charge, its hem billows softly, as if by a dramatic breeze. You must be wearing the coat to use its properties, which use a save DC of 17.  
  
𝙁𝙪𝙢𝙞𝙜𝙖𝙩𝙚. As a bonus action, you can expend 1 of the coat’s charges to duplicate the effects of the “misty step” spell from it. When you do, a cloud of thick fumes heavily obscures the area you left in a 10-foot-radius sphere, centered on a point within 5 feet of your original space. The fumes dissipate at the start of your next turn.  
  
𝙎𝙩𝙚𝙖𝙢 𝘽𝙪𝙧𝙨𝙩. As an action, you can expend 2 of the coat’s charges to release a wave of powerful steam. Each creature within 20 feet of you (other than yourself) must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save, a creature takes 6d6 fire damage and is pushed 10 feet away from you. On a successful save, a creature takes half as much damage and isn’t pushed. Being underwater doesn’t grant resistance against this damage.  
  
𝙑𝙚𝙣𝙤𝙢𝙤𝙪𝙨 𝙃𝙖𝙯𝙚. As an action, you can expend 3 of the coat’s charges to duplicate the effects of the “cloudkill” spell, centered on a point within 5 feet of you. This version of the spell doesn’t require your concentration, and it creates a 30-foot-radius sphere of fog, instead of its normal size. It remains fixed on the point for the duration or until it’s dispersed by wind. You can see normally within the cloud created by this spell.
