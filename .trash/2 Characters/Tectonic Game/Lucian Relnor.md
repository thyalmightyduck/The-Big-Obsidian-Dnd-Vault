# Backstory
Lucian Relnor was never suited for the cruel pragmatism of the Underdark. While his peers practiced the blade or the web-spun politics of Drow society, Lucian sought refuge in the quiet corners of his settlement's archives. He was a creature of ink and parchment, fueled by a curiosity that his parents warned was a death sentence. To the Drow, the surface world was a myth of burning light and weakness, but to Lucian, it was the ultimate unanswered question
His ambition nearly ended in the mandibles of an Umber Hulk. While venturing into the "Safe Zone" fringes to find a rumored cache of scrolls, the beast ambushed him. Lucian survived only by blind luck and a desperate scramble through a collapsed tunnel, leaving him shaken and acutely aware of his own frailty. He realized then that mere book-learning wouldn't get him to the surface; he needed a key to unlock the path.
Years later, while excavating a ruin that predated even the oldest Drow records, he found it: a tome bound in a material that felt like cold, cured silk. It didn't contain letters, but shifting geometries that seemed to move when he blinked. He felt a sort of strange calling, a calling to open up that book and gain the forbidden knowledge inside. The "calling" wasn't a voice-it was a pressure in his skull, a vacuum demanding to be filled.
When Lucian opened the book, the dam broke. He didn't just read the knowledge; he endured it. He saw the birth of stars and the shivering silence of the Great Old One, a being that existed before the gods. The pact was less a handshake and more a parasitic bond: the entity granted Lucian the arcana to survive the trek upward, and in exchange, Lucian became a living vessel for secrets that would drive a lesser mind to madness.
Armed with these flickering, alien powers, Lucian turned his back on the Underdark. He braved the light he had only ever read about, emerging onto the surface not as the scholar he was, but as something new and something much more dangerous.
