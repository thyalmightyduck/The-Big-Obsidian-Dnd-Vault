# Species Traits:
## The Mithral Canvas
**Creature Type:** Humanoid
**Size:** Your size if medium. (about 7-9 feet tall)
**Speed:** You walking speed is 30 feet, and you have a swimming speed equal to your walking speed.

**Marine Dweller:** You can hold your breath for up to 1 hour, and you have resistance to cold damage.
**Walesong:** You can speak in musical tones to communicate simple ideas to other cetaceans that can hear you. While underwater, it can be heard by other cetaceans within the same body of water up to 1,000 feet away.

### Nomadic Giants of the Seas
**Ocean Ancestry:** Your ancestry grants you special traits. Choose a lineage from the Ocean Lineages below and gain the respective benefit:

#### Monodon
 Monodon Cetaceans are smaller and more graceful than their larger kin. they have bulbous melon at the top of their head, which helps store magical energy when concentrating.

For your Dexterity and Intelligence, increase one score by 2 and increase the other score by 1, to a maximum of 20.

Your walking speed in increased by 5 feet.

When you fail a constitution saving throw to maintain concentration on a spell, you can use your reaction to reroll the die, and you must use the new roll. You can use this reaction a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

#### Orcinus
Orcinus Cetaceans are the strong and talented hunters of their kin, with innate senses that help to track down prey/

For your Strength and Charisma, increase on score by 2 and increase the other score by 1, to a maximum of 20.

You have advantage on Survival checks to track down enemies that are missing hit points.

As a bonus action, you gain blindsight with a range of 30 feet for 10 minutes. While you are underwater this range in increased to 60 feet. You can use this bonus action a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

#### Baleen
Baleen cetacean are the most massive of their kin, towering over most other species  and sporting a thick resilient hide. They can store large amounts of air and water in their bodies, releasing it in a forceful black from their  blowholes.

For you Constitution and Wisdom, increase one score by 2 and increase the other score by 1, to a maximum of 20. 

You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push a drag. Additionally, you have advantage on Strength saving throws to resist being moved against your will.

As an action, you can spray a cone of water from your blowhole. The range of the cone is equal to 5 times your Constitution modifier. Each creature in the area must make a Strength saving throw (DC 8 + your Constitution modifier+ you proficiency bonus). On a failed save, a creature is moved 10 feet away from you and knocked prone, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

# Info
## The Mithral Canvas
Descended from the mighty whales that roam the waves, Cetaceans are amongst the most elusive of the many species in the realms. Keen to keep their communities away form prying eyes and potential dangers, they reside in secluded pods for safety until coming of age, at which point they venture into the world to gain their own life experiences.

Depending on their ancestral origins, Cetaceans can vary greatly in physicality. Some appear with hulking frames beyond an average humanoid, while others are fleet of foot with more slender builds. Regardless of these variances, Cetaceans all share an innate connection to the ocean and exhibit abilities passes down from their ancestors.
