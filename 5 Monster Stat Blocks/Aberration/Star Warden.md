# **<u>Traits</u>**  
**Alien Intellect.** The sentient planetoid is immune to any effect that would sense its emotions or read its thoughts, as well as any divination spell that it refuses. Wisdom (Insight) checks made to ascertain the sentient planetoid's intentions or sincerity have [[Disadvantage]].

**Cosmic Aura.** A creature that starts its turn within 30 feet of the star warden is subjected to the following effect. _Charisma Saving Throw:_ DC 17. _Failure:_ The target is hurled through time and space, witnessing every moment of existence in a single second. It takes 17 (5d6) `dice:5d6` Psychic damage and is teleported to an unoccupied space it can see within 30 feet.

**Delerium Healing.** As an action, the star warden can touch a delerium shard to regain 10 (3d6) `dice:3d6` [[Hit Point]]. Once the star warden has used a delerium shard in this way, the shard can't be used in this manner again for 24 hours.

**Fully Contaminated.** The star warden is immune to [[Contamination]] and has [[Advantage]] on saving throws against contaminated spells. The star warden may cast contaminated spells even though it does not gain [[Contamination]] levels.

**Lords of Time and Space.** When the star warden is reduced to 0 [[Hit Point]], roll a d20. On a 1 or 20, the star warden regains all of its [[Hit Point]] and another creature of its choice within 30 feet is reduced to 0 [[Hit Point]] instead.
# <u>Actions</u> 
**Multiattack.** The star warden makes two attacks.

**Staff of Stars.** _Melee or Ranged Attack Roll:_ +8, reach 5 ft. or range 60/120 ft. _Hit:_ 22 (5d6 + 4) `dice:5d6+4` Radiant damage plus 10 (3d6) `dice:3d6` Force damage.

**Nebulae Grasp.** _Melee Attack Roll:_ +8, reach 5 ft. _Hit:_ 27 (5d8 + 4) `dice:5d8+4` Piercing damage, and the star warden can teleport the target to an unoccupied space the star warden can see within 120 feet.

**Stardust.** _Dexterity Saving Throw:_ DC 17, each creature in a 30-foot [[Cone]]. _Failure:_ 18 (4d6 + 4) `dice:4d6+4` Radiant damage and the target has the [[Burning]] condition (4d6 Radiant) `dice:4d6`.

# <u>Bonus Actions</u> 
**The Cold Void of Space.** _Constitution Saving Throw:_ DC 17, one creature within 120 feet of the star warden that teleported this turn. _Failure:_ The target has the [[Frozen]] condition. _Success:_ 10 (3d6) `dice:3d6` Cold damage.

# <u>Reactions</u> 
**Outside Time and Space.** _Trigger:_ A creature hits the star warden with an attack. _Response:_ The star warden switches places with another creature within 30 feet, and the attack hits that creature instead.

# **<u>Lore</u>** 
A massive twelve-foot-tall creature stands before you draped in ragged and tattered cloth. The cloth covers its form except for four gnarled and gangly gray arms. Its twisted hands end in four long fingers. They clutch a wooden staff that sparkles with radiating cosmic energy. Two spindley gray legs stick out from under the cloak exposing three-toed feet. Its hunched form leans its weight onto the staff. The bulbous mass of cloth implies a massive head below the cloak, tilted forward. The cloth is filled with writhing tentacles, which dangle out from its depths, poking and prodding about.

The Star Wardens are ancient creatures who have traversed the cosmos for centuries. They care little for the multiple realities and their squabbles, nor the relevance of a mortal life. They are eternal beings who exist out of space and time. To them, relativity and dimensions are broken concepts which they can bend to their whims. They exist in multiple dimensions and times at once. They move and age in counterintuitive ways that no mortal can fully comprehend. To a mortal eye, they may appear to phase in and out of existence, or teleport from place to place, at times appearing more ancient, or more youthful. The Star Wardens may not be teleporting at all, but simply experiencing the interaction with a mortal at multiple points in time and space simultaneously. Their existence is wholly outside the standard flow of time or the movement of spatial reasoning. A Star Warden may exist within the past present and future of a mortal being all at the same time.

Their physiology and bizarre existence is utterly baffling to a mortal observer. The few who have witnessed a Star Warden have gone mad by the sheer incomprehensibility of their existence. One such academy mage, Randolf Carter, who practiced entering the Space Between Worlds via Dreamland, said that at several points throughout his life, he has spoken to a Star Warden. From the Star Warden's perspective, their conversation was a singular event, yet for Randolf, it happened in tiny dream-like snippets over the course of 50 years.

The Star Wardens shepherd the cosmos, observing and aiding in the flow of space and time. They view reality as a wavelength of interdimensional strands, and they see to the various timelines and branches of infinite realities. It is impossible to know the full scope of their purpose, but they seem to be protectors of timelines, and ensure no cataclysmic events cause the universe to collapse on itself.

### Study the Foe
Characters proficient in History or Nature gain [[Advantage]] on Intelligence (Arcana) checks when they take the [[Study action]] to examine a Star Warden, learning the following based on the result.

(10+) Mentally Tough. You know the creature's [[Immunity]].

(15+) Transposition. You recognize the creature's Outside Time and Space [[Reaction]].

(20+) Otherworldly Intellect. You learn the creature's highest and lowest ability scores.

### Combat Tactics
Star wardens exist in multiple times and dimensions at once. A creature that tries to fight a star warden is at a loss for where and how the star warden exists before it. Both the Star Warden and the creatures facing it are constantly shifted around the battlefield, unable to grasp their sense of reality. The Star Warden uses its Outside Time and Space Reaction to let two creatures attack one another instead of landing a blow upon itself. It can force the truth of all reality into a creature's mind or have it experience the cold vastness of space. Star Wardens are clever and always flee a losing fight.
